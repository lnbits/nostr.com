All Kinds of Stuff

Like the internet itself: open and chaotic

Nostr embraces the chaos of the early internet‚Äîmultiple kinds of data, diverse forms of user interaction and different clients providing their own perspectives over the same underlying information.

A new paradigm for communication

In Nostr, every message carries a digital signature that proves its authorship and authenticity without any need for any third-party authority or external checks. It's this foundation of trust that enables the decentralized transport and broadcasting of information.

Read a short, human-friendly explanation ‚Ä∫

Cryptographically signed notes

Nostr's single unit of information is a cryptographically signed note, these are created by users in their client software and published to one or more relays.

Smart clients as user agents

Clients are smart and act as agents for the users who install them. They decide which relays to connect to and when and what data to request according to the circumstance and user preferences.

Relays as distribution centres

These are the servers that notes are published to and read from. They cannot change the contents of notes (that would invalidate the signature), but they can decide what to store and for how long.

Exploring a new paradigm

Besides being a natural medium for a Twitter-like microblogging social network, Nostr can also be used for other purposes. And not only similar things like sharing videos, longform articles, pictures or voice notes. There are initiatives on Nostr for the construction of sub-protocols that power closed groups, decentralized wikipedia, couchsurfing, geocaching or web annotations; as well as protocols that don't use Nostr for the core data but as a coordination and discovery mechanism, such as decentralized code collaboration using git, file hosting, torrent sharing and video livestreaming.

How Nostr works

Nostr enables true freedom by allowing users to stay connected to their audience even in adverse scenarios.

User chooses their outbox relays

Patricia publishes a special event announcing which servers she will use as her "outbox relay" for publishing notes. She then starts sending all her posts to these chosen relays, creating a predictable place where others can find her content.

Because servers are interchangeable any will suffice.

Someone starts following

When Flora wants to read Patricia's posts, his client looks up her announcement and connects directly to her announced outbox relays.

This targeted approach is simple and efficient.

Users can switch relays when needed

If Patricia's current relay bans her or shuts down, she can easily switch to different relays ‚Äî perhaps some run by friends, a paid service, or her own server.

She simply publishes a new announcement and continues to post without losing her audience.

Clients track relay changes

Flora's client continuously monitors for updates to Patricia's relay list.

When she switches relays, his client eventually starts querying her new locations, ensuring he never misses her posts even as the network topology changes.

Smart clients manage everything

From the publisher side to the follower side clients behave smartly, keeping a local state and reacting to new information in order to ensure that the flow of information continues.

After all, these notes are important, it would be sad to miss too many of them.

I've got some questions!

It may sound like Nostr is very good, but what about these hard issues?

How does Nostr handle spam and unwanted content?

Nostr itself, as a protocol, does not have built-in spam filtering or moderation because it is designed to be censorship-resistant and decentralized. Instead, spam control is delegated to users and relay operators, creating a distributed approach to content management.

Users can choose which relays to connect to based on their spam policies, and relays can implement their own filtering rules to block or limit spammy content. Additionally, users can block or ignore public keys that send spam. This decentralized approach means spam management is distributed and customizable but requires active participation by users and relay operators.

Will Nostr scale effectively with massive user adoption?

While the protocol is designed to be horizontally scalable via multiple relays, the practical limits of relay network size, data synchronization, and latency under heavy load remain uncertain. The distributed nature should theoretically allow for better scaling than centralized platforms.

However, challenges include ensuring data consistency across relays, managing bandwidth costs for relay operators, and maintaining performance as the network grows. The success of scaling will largely depend on the development of efficient relay implementations and economic incentives for operators to maintain high-quality infrastructure.

What protections does Nostr offer against online harassment?

Since Nostr identities are just public keys without centralized control, preventing harassment is challenging. There is no central authority to ban or suspend users, which means traditional moderation approaches don't apply.

Instead, users must rely on personal blocking and muting of offending public keys. Relay operators can also choose to block or filter abusive content on their servers. The decentralized nature means harassment cannot be universally prevented, but users can curate their own experience by selecting trusted relays and blocking harassers. This model emphasizes user control but requires vigilance and community cooperation.

Why not just use Mastodon?

Nostr eliminates the server dependency issues that plague Mastodon's federation model. In Mastodon, if your server goes down or the admin decides to shut it down, you lose your identity, followers, and content. With Nostr, your identity is cryptographic and portable - you can switch relays without losing anything.

Additional advantages over Mastodon include:

  • No need to choose a server or worry about server politics
  • Built-in micropayments and monetization through Bitcoin integration
  • Simpler protocol without complex federation rules
  • Resistance to defederation and server-level censorship
  • Lower barrier to entry for running infrastructure

Mastodon currently has better moderation tools, a more mature ecosystem, and established communities. However, Nostr's architecture provides stronger guarantees against censorship and platform lock-in, making it more resilient for long-term use.

Why not just use Bluesky?

Nostr offers true decentralization compared to Bluesky's more controlled federation model. While Bluesky uses the AT Protocol which still relies on centralized components like identity servers and relies heavily on a single company's infrastructure, Nostr has no central points of failure or control. Users can switch relays instantly without losing their identity or social connections.

Key advantages include:

  • No corporate control or potential for platform capture
  • Simpler protocol that's easier to implement and understand
  • Direct integration with Bitcoin and Lightning Network
  • True data portability - your identity and data aren't tied to any specific service

However, Bluesky currently offers better user experience, more polished clients, and easier onboarding for mainstream users. The trade-off is between Nostr's radical decentralization and Bluesky's more user-friendly but centralized approach.

How will content discovery and search work without centralized indexing?

Without centralized search engines, content discovery relies on specialized relays that provide indexing services, client-side search capabilities, and social signals like recommendations from followed users. This creates a more fragmented but potentially more diverse discovery landscape.

Comprehensive search across the entire network remains a significant challenge, as it requires either massive indexing efforts by individual relays or new protocols for distributed search. Users may need to rely more on social discovery, hashtags, and curated lists rather than algorithmic recommendations.

Is Nostr related to Bitcoin?

Nostr and Bitcoin share fundamental design principles and are deeply complementary. Both use cryptographic keys for identity, prioritize decentralization and censorship resistance, and were created by pseudonymous developers who stepped back from direct control. Nostr uses the same secp256k1 elliptic curve cryptography as Bitcoin, making it natural to integrate Bitcoin wallets and Lightning Network payments directly into social applications.

The relationship manifests in several ways:

  • Native Lightning Network integration for instant micropayments and tips
  • Bitcoin wallet compatibility for identity management
  • Shared cryptographic standards and security models
  • Similar ethos of individual sovereignty and resistance to censorship
  • Complementary technologies - Bitcoin for money, Nostr for communication

Many see Nostr as the social layer that Bitcoin was missing, enabling value transfer and communication to work together seamlessly. This creates new possibilities for creator monetization, micropayments for content, and building economic relationships directly into social interactions without intermediaries taking cuts or controlling the flow of information and money.
